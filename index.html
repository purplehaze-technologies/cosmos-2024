About COSMOS

Naval Physical and Oceanographic Laboratory (NPOL), Kochi is a premier laboratory of Defence Research and Development Organization (DRDO) which was established in 1952. Over the years, NPOL has engaged in the development of SOund Navigation And Ranging (SONAR) and allied technologies. The thrust areas of R&D of NPOL are Oceanography, Transducers, Materials, Engineering systems, Electronics and Signal processing relevant to Anti Submarine Warfare. In recent years, NPOL has also ventured in to niche area of non - acoustic detection. Oceanographic research at NPOL has been mainly focused on observations and modeling studies of oceanic processes to understand the ocean environment and its role in Design, Development and Evaluation of Underwater Sensors. Performance of these sensors is highly influenced by the spatio-temporal variability of the ocean environment. Recent worldwide shifting of focus by various defence agencies to comprehensive Underwater Domain Awareness (UDA) of their respective regions calls for a systematic scientific deliberation and assessment capabilities in this area. NPOL had conducted first edition of COSMOS in 2013 which had played an important role in formulation of oceanographic projects of national importance. Continuing with the series of symposium, NPOL is hosting COSMOS-2024 with a focused theme - “Coastal Oceanography: An Underwater Domain Awareness Perspective” during 17-19 April 2024. A pre-symposium workshop on ASW Oceanography will be conducted for the registered students by scientists of NPOL on 17 April 2024. The Symposium is expected to provide a forum for scientists and technologists working in the relevant fields to exchange ideas. This would immensely help all the stakeholders to plan further research and studies in the deeper perspective of underwater domain awareness.

Extended abstracts (3-4 pages) are invited on selected topics related to the theme of the symposium.
